While you’re moving throughout the city, WiFind will be collecting some information about your location and phone in order to help out our researchers. To do that, we’ll need to collect some data about your movements and device while you’re moving throughout the city. You can turn the collection off at any point by tapping the “Enable Scanning” button.

The app works in the background by scanning for Wi-Fi networks in your area as you move throughout the city. It measures signal strengths for all of the networks by their service set identifier (SSID), or the name you see when you try to connect.

Once you connect to Wi-Fi, the app uploads the data you’ve collected and can then be seen on the map located on the WiFind website. We didn’t want to use up your mobile data plan, so the app will never upload what you’ve collected until you’re connected to Wi-Fi.

We will be collecting some data about your phone while you’re mapping Wi-Fi signals, but don’t worry; it’s all in the name of science! We take your privacy very seriously and have done our best to ensure your data is safe. The data we collect falls into three categories: Location, Network, and Device.
